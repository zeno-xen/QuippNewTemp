Sherry Engle

Sherry Engle is an author, scholar, and advocate for women's contributions to American theater history. Her work includes New Women Dramatists in America, 1890-1920 which focuses on reclaiming the voices of pioneering female playwrights who challenged societal norms and contributed to Progressive Era theater. She co-edited with Susan Croft Thousands of Noras: Short Plays by Women,1875-1920, an international collection of various, often "undiscovered" works by women that range from comedy to social commentary.


Dr. Engle's research shines a light on playwrights who have long been overlooked. Her writing not only documents the vibrant and often radical theatrical work of women in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, but also situates their influence within a broader movement for gender and artistic equality.
